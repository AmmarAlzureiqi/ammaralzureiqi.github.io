Exploring and Comparing the Experience of COVID 19 Accross the Globe

Every country experienced covid differently. They spiked in cases at different times, some countries reacted better than others
and some made better decisions than others. In this vizualisation we hope to explore the differences between countries.

Exploring COVID-19 Globally

We first visualize the growth in number of new covid cases and tests from Jan 3, 2020 to July 26, 2023.
Pressing 'Play' or moving the slider will take you through the dates showing the growth of COVID 19 by date. The drop-down menu can also be used to filter by continent (hovering a point will display the country name).
You may notice that some continents were better than others at testing during certain time periods. It is hard to make any solid generalizations as each country and continent experienced covid differently and spiked at different times.

World View

Here we explore the number of new cases reported worldwide from Jan 3, 2020 to July 26, 2023. The various waves that occured over the pandemic have been labelled. The number of cases spiked after each one.
Hovering any where on the chart will display a tool tip that indicates the number of cases reported and the date. By using the drop down-menu, you can add another value to the tooltip that indicates the number of cases reported by a specific country. We can see how many cases on a certain day were contributed by a selected country.
It is important to note that the large spike toward the end of the chart is due to a mass reporting of cases from china at the end of the pandemic, and not a true increase in cases

COVID-19

Here we compare the spread by date accross two countries. (We can notice right away that China did not report any cases until the end of 2022 and that is why we have a large spike in the data)
The intention of this slide is for the viewer to select two different countries and compare the charts. You might see that the charts of some countries will vary greatly from others. Countries experience spikes ('break outs') at different times.
When living in a country throughout the whole pandemic, it's easy to think everyone accross the world experienced it the same way. This is definately not the cases, some countries didn't lockdown as much, others were able to aquire vaccines earlier, etc.
